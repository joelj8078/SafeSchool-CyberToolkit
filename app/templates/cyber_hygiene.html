Cyber Hygiene Tips (Non-Technical)

  • Use strong, unique passwords for each account.
  • Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) wherever possible.
  • Be cautious of unsolicited emails and links.
  • Regularly update your apps and devices.
  • Install and update antivirus/anti-malware software.
  • Back up important data regularly to the cloud or external drive.
  • Do not share personal information on unknown websites or social media.
  • Use a secure, password-protected Wi-Fi connection.

Cyber Hygiene Guidelines (Technical)

  • Apply OS and security patches promptly (use unattended-upgrades on Linux).
  • Enforce principle of least privilege for user and system permissions.
  • Implement secure coding practices to prevent OWASP Top 10 vulnerabilities.
  • Configure secure SSH settings (disable root login, enforce key-based auth).
  • Use centralized logging and SIEM for monitoring suspicious behavior.
  • Perform regular vulnerability scans and penetration testing.
  • Use firewalls, IDS/IPS and enforce network segmentation.
  • Encrypt sensitive data at rest and in transit (e.g., TLS, full-disk encryption).
